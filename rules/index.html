Festival Rules

Registration

The Art Registration form must be completed and received by noon, February 14, 2009. Registration opens online January 1, 2009. Contact Lawan Andersen after January 1 if you do not have internet access to register online. No incomplete entry form will be accepted. A digital image must be attached to the registration form to be used for identification purposes only. The piece does not have to be completely finished for the registration form but must be recognizable in the photo. The artist's statement must also be completed and submitted with the registration form.

Criteria

Artwork will be juried after delivery to be accepted into the show. Criteria: artistic merit and appropriateness to theme as also reflected in the artist's statement. Works must score highly in both categories to win.

Art Delivery Day

Artwork will be received on Art Delivery Day, March 28, 2009. If a work is judged to not fit the theme, PCPC will eliminate it upon registration or the day of delivery and the artist can take it home. All the delivered work will be juried from March 30 to April 3, 2009. A $25.00 entry fee (cash or check) is required for each artist (or group of artists) and will be collected on Art Delivery Day.

Limitations

Artists must be 16 years of age or older. Due to space limitations, only two entries will be allowed per person or group of artists. A limited number of artwork pieces will be chosen for exhibition during the jury process.

Acceptable Media

Acceptable media categories are original, handmade works on canvas, water media on paper, pastels, drawings, fabric, sculpture, and photography. Photography entrants must acknowledge that the work is an original single work created solely by the entrant and that no infringement of copyright exists. The original unmodified camera image must be submitted. A photograph that has elements removed is not eligible. No giclée prints.

Presentation requirements

Two-dimensional works must be appropriately framed (no clip-framed work is acceptable), ready for wall mounting, and must not exceed 42 inches in any direction (including framing). Gallery-wrapped canvas with no exposed staples or tacks will be accepted only if it is properly wired for hanging and all exposed canvas is painted. Sculpture footprint must not exceed 2 feet x 3 feet by 40 inches height. A stand must be provided by the artist and fit within the footprint.

*The exhibition reserves the right to exclude any entry that exceeds size limitations, presents unusual installation problems, cannot be handled by exhibit volunteers, is improperly framed, or is damaged upon receipt. No unfinished or wet works will be accepted. All works must be received by designated delivery date and in time for consideration in the show and an award.

Delivery Requirements

Artists are to deliver their entry in person on March 28, 2009, 9:00 am - 1:00 pm to 4101 Oak Lawn Avenue. Volunteers will be on hand to assist artists with unloading their work. No work of art will be accepted after that date and time.

Sale by Consignment and Removal from Premises

Unless agreed prior to registration, all pieces entered are to be available for sale by consignment with the artist receiving 75% and PCPC receiving 25% to help defray show costs. Unsold pieces must be picked up on May 4, 2009, 9:00 am - 1:00 pm. If you personally are not able to pick up your work, please make arrangements for that day and advise PCPC as to who will be picking it up. Only unforeseeable, serious exceptions will be considered for early or delayed pick-up alternatives.

Awards

The top works in each category will be honored with a ribbon. The "Best of Theme" and "Viewers' Choice" winners will each receive a cash award of $1,000.
